Research Background

My PhD research focuses on Machine Learning for Computational Imaging, with a particular interest in Attention Mechanisms for Image Restoration. I am the first author of several publications in top venues, including ICML, IEEE TCI and IEEE ISBI, and a contributing author on works published in JMLR and NeurIPS Workshops. I have received multiple awards for presenting my work during my PhD. I have extensive experience with deep learning and data analysis in Python, particularly using PyTorch, as well as scientific computing libraries such as NumPy, SciPy, scikit-learn, and pandas. I am familiar with parallel computing and GPU programming using CUDA. My work is grounded in a strong theoretical background in mathematics, statistics, and signal processing.

Career Vision

I'm interested in using machine learning to solve real-world problems. I’m especially excited about diffusion models and attention-based architectures, and I want to continue working on these in a broader ML context. I'm drawn to problems where modeling and domain knowledge come together—whether it’s in vision, science, or anything that involves reasoning from complex data. I care about building models that are not just accurate, but also interpretable and practical in the real world.
